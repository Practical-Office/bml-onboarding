Module 7 · ~30 min

Your first project

Ship a real experiment issue in sandbox — practice the full template without polluting the live board. A lead graduates it when the hypothesis and thresholds are sound.

7

Why

Sandbox before the live board

~30 minutes

Reading about the Build–Measure–Learn Loop is not the same as writing a falsifiable experiment ticket. Module 7 is the mandatory exercise: create your first real issue using the full template, with numeric pass and kill thresholds, a named measure path, and a duration.

Practice issues stay in sandbox until a lead promotes them. That protects the operating board’s WIP limit (max three active in Build+Measure combined) and keeps active experiments honest. Leads check template completeness and threshold quality before graduation — not whether you “like” the idea.

Hard truth: If your sandbox issue could not survive lead review, it should not have been on the live board yet.

7

How

Create, submit, graduate

Step 1 — Create the sandbox issue

  1. Open your product repo (or teaching repo for practice) → New issue → Build Loop Experiment template.
  2. Fill every section: hypothesis (one sentence), Build (smallest test), Measure table (pass + kill + how collected), duration, sample.
  3. Apply label experiment.
  4. Do not add the issue to the live Project board until a lead graduates it.

Step 2 — Post your submission

Share the issue URL where your lead specifies (repo Discussion, Slack, or team channel):

Build Loop Module 7 submission
Issue: https://github.com/YOUR-ORG/YOUR-REPO/issues/NNN
Hypothesis (one line): …

Step 3 — Lead graduation

Your lead reviews:

  • Complete template with falsifiable hypothesis
  • Numeric pass and kill criteria plus duration
  • Named measure path (instrumentation or manual log)
  • Fit under the three-active Build+Measure cap when promoted

When approved, the lead adds the issue to the live board at Backlog (or moves an existing draft). If sandbox-only practice is enough for your role, the lead confirms that on the certification checklist.

BookIQ teammates: create the sandbox issue on Book-IQ/bookiqv1-rc; lead graduates it to the Build Loop Experiments board when ready. See the BookIQ appendix for week-1 draft examples.

Failure modes

  • Live board pollution — adding practice tickets to the operating Project before review. Crowds WIP and trains the team to ignore Backlog discipline.
  • Vague hypothesis — “Improve onboarding” is not falsifiable. Name customer behavior you expect to see.
  • Missing kill criteria — without a numeric fail line, Learn becomes vibes-only theater.
  • Skipping submission — certification requires a posted link; silent sandbox issues do not count.

Module 7 — progress checklist

Module 7 — self-check

  1. Why sandbox before adding to the live board?
  2. What must a lead check before graduating your issue?
  3. What happens if Learn never produces kill or pivot decisions?

Try answering aloud before opening the answers.

Answers
  1. So practice tickets do not crowd the live WIP limit or dilute active experiments. Leads keep the operating board honest.
  2. Complete template, falsifiable hypothesis, numeric pass/kill + duration, measure path named, and that the experiment fits under the three-active Build+Measure cap when promoted.
  3. The loop is theater — shipping without deciding. The Learn column exists to force persevere, pivot, or kill from evidence.
